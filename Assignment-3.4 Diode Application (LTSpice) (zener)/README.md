Does the Zener diode keep the output voltage nearly constant?
Yes, the Zener diode acts as a voltage regulator by maintaining a stable output voltage of approximately $10.22\text{V}$ despite the higher $16\text{V}$ input.

Why?
Reverse Breakdown: The Zener diode is designed to operate in its reverse breakdown region. Once the input voltage exceeds its Zener rating ($10\text{V}$), it allows current to flow while keeping the voltage across it nearly fixed.

Voltage Dropping: The series resistor ($R_1$) absorbs the difference between the source voltage ($16\text{V}$) and the Zener voltage ($10\text{V}$), protecting the circuit and stabilizing the output.

Steep Characteristic: In breakdown, the diode's I-V curve is very steep, meaning even if the current through the diode changes, the voltage remains almost the same.
